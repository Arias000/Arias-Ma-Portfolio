LEVEE LANDSCAPE DESIGN IN HANGZHOU FUTURE CITY PRACTICE AREA

Hangzhou, China

Service

Landscape Architecture, Master Planning

Sector

Ecological Levee

Client

—

Size

4.083 square kilometers (k㎡)

Overview

Challenge

Inadequate Flood Protection Standards: The current levees (such as the Shangsi and Coral Sand embankments) only meet 20-to-50-year flood standards, falling short of the required 100-year protection level. This poses a significant inundation risk to the region.

Ecological Fragmentation: Existing hard embankments physically isolate the city from the Qiantang River. This disconnection has led to blocked ecological corridors and a loss of natural habitats for migratory birds and benthic organisms (such as the zebra frog).

Monotonous Infrastructure & Poor Connectivity: The current riverfront is dominated by motor vehicle traffic with a fragmented slow-traffic system, resulting in a low-quality public experience and a lack of urban vitality.

Solution

Resilient Design & "Wave Hills" Topography: The project adopts a "Wave Hills" concept, elevating levee heights (to 11-18m) and using S-shaped symmetrical designs to enhance structural stability against extreme climate events and sea-level rise.

"One Brush" Masterplan with Five Thematic Zones: The 26km shoreline is transformed into five distinct functional zones based on hydrological conditions: Urban Vitality, Future Art, Leisure Recreation, Poetic Farmland, and Joy-Living.

Ecological Restoration via "Silt Management": Utilizing dredged silt from the Qiantang River to construct landforms ("Building Mountains"), the design restores tidal flat wetlands and creates "life corridors" with climbing platforms for benthic species.

Integrated Smart Levee & Slow-Traffic System: A 26km multi-level slow-traffic network (walking, cycling, and running paths) is integrated with a Smart Levee system, featuring AR interaction, autonomous transit, and sensor-based environmental monitoring

Result

The "Hangzhou Sample" for Future Cities: The project serves as a core carrier for the "Future City Practice Area," demonstrating a modern model of harmonious coexistence between humanity and nature.

Long-term Safety & Biodiversity: The upgraded infrastructure significantly extends the levee's lifespan and eliminates breach risks while providing resilient ecological habitats that support local and migratory species

Urban Vitality & Regional Value Enhancement: By adopting a "Landscape-First" development model, the project transforms the levee into a high-quality public realm, attracting high-net-worth talent and fostering a vibrant gateway for culture, art, and healthy living
