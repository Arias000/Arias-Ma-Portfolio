PANLONGSHAN PARK

Jiangyin, Jiangsu Province

Service

Landscape Architecture

Sector

Academic

Client

—

Size

Approx. 69.30 ha

Overview

The project is titled “REBIRTH OF MOUNTAIN” and is located in the High-tech Zone of Jiangyin City, Jiangsu Province, China, where it enjoys a superior geographical location south of the Yangtze River and west of the Jiangyin city center. With a total area of approximately 69.30 hectares, it is positioned as the central park of the High-tech Zone.

Its core design philosophy uses mountain body regeneration as a starting point, integrating ecological, landscape, educational, and entertainment functions to restore the mountain’s ecological chain. While reviving natural vitality, the project also seeks to stimulate economic growth in the surrounding areas, ultimately achieving a co-evolution between city development and the mountain.

Challenge

Mountain Destruction: The continuous expansion of the city has led to the gradual reduction and shrinking of the mountain. Long-term human activities, such as mining and quarrying, have severely damaged the mountain structure, resulting in numerous cliffs (some with slopes exceeding 70°) and damaged facades.

Environmental Pollution: The process of industrialization resulted in a large number of factories on-site, generating severe heavy metal pollution, chemical pollution, rain runoff pollution, and industrial emission pollution, all of which have affected the soil and the mountain.

Ecological Fragmentation: The urbanization process has led to landscape fragmentation and a reduction in animal habitats. There is currently a lack of effective ecological connectivity between the mountain and the urban interface.

Complex Terrain: While there are flat areas within the site, the middle section is lined with cliffs, making restoration highly difficult. The total area requiring repair reaches 10,000 square meters.

Solution

Multidimensional Mountain Restoration Strategy:

Slope Restoration: Targeted measures are taken based on different gradients. The “Swallow Nest Restoration” method is used for slopes below 40°; the “Injection by layer with materials” (layered injection of substrate) is applied to slopes between 40° and 70°; for cliffs above 70°, restoration is achieved by digging living troughs (planting tanks) or building floating platforms.

Soil Restoration: This involves a combination of physical repair (peeling and covering polluted soil), chemical repair (applying soil conditioners or reagents), and biological repair (utilizing microbes and specific plants to reduce the concentration of pollutants).

Plant Restoration: A long-term vegetation restoration strategy has been developed to promote the reconstruction of the biological community through artificial means over a 40-year period, transitioning from herbs to shrubs, and ultimately to trees.

Water Systems and Biological Corridors:

Establish a water purification system that includes ecological grass ditches, aquatic plant purification zones, and rain wetlands.

Construct a Biological Corridor to connect scattered animal habitats, reduce landscape fragmentation, and maintain the dynamic ecological processes of nature.

Functional Integration and Spatial Layout:

Education & Arts Plaza: Existing factories are rebuilt and repurposed for this area. It features the cultivation of aromatic plants for essential oil extraction, serving both scientific research and recuperative educational functions.

Exhibition Underground Architecture: This facility displays the formation and damaging process of the mountain while popularizing restoration methods and ecological benefits.

System Classification: Various systems—including activity spaces, roads, water systems, greenlands, wetlands, and farmland—are connected in series to form a mutually supportive and complementary ecological network.

Result

Ecological Resurgence: The project successfully restored the damaged mountain body and the mountain ecological chain. It improved soil quality by reducing the concentration of pollutants, such as heavy metals and chemical substances, in the soil.

Urban Function Enhancement: It provides the High-tech Zone with a valuable central park that integrates leisure, education, and culture. Key features include the Eco-Vitality Lawn, which serves as a recreation and ecological transition space, and the Half of Mountain Lake.

Social and Economic Benefits: By using mountain body regeneration as a starting point, the project has stimulated the economic growth of the surrounding areas. It has successfully achieved a state of co-evolution between city development and the mountain.

Scientific Research and Educational Value: The project established a platform, such as the Exhibition Underground Architecture, to show the formation and damaging process of the mountain while popularizing restoration methods and ecological benefits to the public.
